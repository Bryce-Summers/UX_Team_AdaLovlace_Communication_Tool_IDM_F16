Research

The primary research inspiration was based on the beautiful design of the Google Tilt Brush and the Japan Airlines HoloLens Experience. As our prototype evolved, the team researched other navigation opportunities in 3D Modeling software such as Maya and SolidWorks. Consequently, one of functionalities, the “NavTree,” resembles the SolidWorks tree map navigation. As for our second option, the team went beyond software and discovered the interconnectivity of the Lego toys to be a true inspiration for the “explosion mode” of ExploSphere.

About

This project was made in conjunction with the NASA JPL Ops Lab and the User Experience Course at NYU Tandon School of Engineering

ExploSphere

The ExploSphere is a new augment, whose main purpose is to provide an exploded view of a user-selected object. The exploded object, exploded one hierarchy level down, appears within a translucent sphere that distinguishes it from the parts of the full rover model. The ExploSphere can be dragged to different locations around the user, and can be toggled on and off. Objects in this view can also be selected and pinned.

NavTree

The NavTree is a new augment, whose main purpose is to provide a hierarchical database model view of a user-selected object. The NavTree features a 2D single-level parent view of the selected object, and displays three levels up the hierarchy tree for that object. Gazing at the child-object level enlarges four child objects at a time with the remaining objects represented as squares. Within each enlarged child object is an image of the object, and below the NavTree is a box displaying the full name of the object that is begin gazed at. The NavTree can be dragged to different locations around the user, and can be toggled on and off. Objects in this view can also be selected and pinned.

Hierarchy Slider

Our Hierarchy Slider is a new augment that allows users to slide between levels of hierarchy.
